Basics of Cricket:
For starters, cricket may seem like a great game. But in reality it is quite easy to understand. 
The basics of the game are simple:

The field consists of two teams of 11 players at a time.
Both teams bat alternately, the batting team trying to score more runs than the opposing team before the end of their innings.

The first batting team is called "inning side" and the second side is called "fielding side".

The fielding team tries to get each player out or out of the batting side by catching a ball flying in the air or hitting the stumps or running out when a batsman leaves his crease too soon. , Hits a ball outside his area, hits a ball. What is cricket?
Cricket is a bat and ball game played on one or more days between two teams. The game is played with a hard ball that is rounded and hardened using resin, which makes it much more difficult than the ordinary cricket ball used in informal sports.


A match consists of innings, in which teams alternate between batting and fielding. A team scores runs when the batsman throws the ball out of the game or completes a four-run home run.
